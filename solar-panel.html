Components of a Solar Panel System

Before we delve into the cost, it's important to know that different types of solar panels are available to suit various needs, and their costs vary accordingly.

Solar Panels

  • The heart of the solar panel system, these panels contain special cells that capture sunlight and convert it into direct current (DC) electricity.
  • Each solar panel consists of silicon solar cells covered by a sheet of glass and held together by a metal frame. When sunlight hits the solar cells, it activates electrons, which then flow through the cell.
  • Wires capture this flow of electrons and combine it with the output of other cells within the panel.
  • Solar panels are classified based on their rated power output in Watts, which indicates the power they can produce in 1 peak sun hour. It's important to note that solar panel output is affected by cell operating temperature.
  • They are an optimum choice to power homes, businesses, and even entire communities.

Inverters

Inverters are crucial components in a solar panel system because they convert the DC electricity produced by the solar panels into the AC electricity needed for our appliances and devices.

There are different types of inverters available:
  • String Inverter:

    This type connects multiple solar panels together and converts their collective power.

  • Microinverters:

    They are attached directly to individual solar panels and are more efficient in converting power, though they may come at a higher cost.

  • Power Optimizers:

    These act as a hybrid between microinverters and string inverters. They are installed at each panel and condition the DC electricity before sending it to a centralized inverter instead of converting it directly to AC.

Solar Battery

A solar battery is a crucial component used to store excess electricity generated by solar panels.

A solar battery is a crucial component used to store excess electricity generated by solar panels.

It acts like a backup power source, ensuring you have electricity even during nights, cloudy days, or power outages.

In a grid-tied solar system, a battery might not be necessary, as it can rely on the power grid for electricity. However, for off-grid solar systems that operate independently, a battery is essential to store and use the energy when there isn’t adequate sunlight.

In the solar industry, there are different battery options available, with the two main types being lead-acid and lithium batteries.

Benefits of Solar Panel System

Choosing the right size for your solar panel system depends on your energy consumption, available roof space, and budget. In Australia, a typical solar PV system is around 6.6kWh, which works well for most households. However, larger systems of 8-10 kWh are gaining popularity in the Residential market.

1. Reduced Carbon Footprint:

  • When you switch to solar energy, you make a significant contribution to reducing your carbon footprint.
  • On average, an Australian household consumes about 15,800 kilowatt-hours (kWh) of electricity annually.
  • By using solar panels, the carbon footprint is approximately 20 times lower compared to traditional coal-powered electricity sources.
  • Thus, solar not only saves you money on electricity bills but also makes a positive impact on the environment.

2. Low Maintenance Costs:

  • One of the great advantages of solar energy systems is their minimal maintenance requirements.
  • Regular cleaning, usually a couple of times per year, is typically all that's needed to keep your solar panels performing optimally. Occasional cable maintenance ensures that your solar power system operates at its peak efficiency. If necessary, a specialised solar maintenance company can assist with this task.
  • Most reputable solar panel manufacturers offer warranties that cover 20-25 years, ensuring peace of mind and protection for your investment.
  • Additionally, solar panels have no moving parts, which means wear and tear are minimal, leading to extended durability and reliability.

3. Increased Property Value:

  • Investing in a solar panel system can significantly enhance the value of your property.
  • In today's real estate market, homebuyers are increasingly drawn to energy-efficient homes with sustainable and renewable energy solutions.
  • Research from Domain supports this notion, showing that homes equipped with solar panel systems are valued much higher than those without.
  • This substantial increase in property value makes solar energy not only an eco-friendly choice but also a smart financial investment for homeowners.
