  • How often should I clean my solar panels?

    Solar panels should be cleaned at least twice a year to remove dirt, dust, and debris. In areas with high pollution or frequent bird activity, more frequent cleaning may be necessary.

  • What is included in solar panel maintenance?

    Maintenance typically includes cleaning the panels, inspecting for damage, checking wiring and connections, and ensuring the inverter is functioning properly.

  • Can I clean solar panels myself?

    Yes, you can clean solar panels yourself using a soft brush, water, and mild soap. However, avoid using abrasive materials or high-pressure water, as they can damage the panels.

  • Do solar panels require professional maintenance?

    While regular cleaning can be done by the homeowner, professional maintenance is recommended annually to ensure optimal performance and identify potential issues.

  • What happens if I don’t maintain my solar panels?

    Neglecting maintenance can lead to decreased efficiency, potential damage, and reduced lifespan of your solar panels. Regular care ensures they operate at maximum capacity.
